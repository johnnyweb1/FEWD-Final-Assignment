Inca and the big balloon

The story about a girl who was determined to see the world

Scroll down for the story


The sun was shining but Inca was looking out of the windows with a glum expression on her face. It was near the end of school holidays and she hadn't been able to do anything interesting at all. Mum and Dad both worked at the cafe and unless she wanted to hang out there all day (no thank you!) she was stuck at home. Her best friend Maisie was away all holidays travelling through India with her family. She kept sending pictures of all these amazing places she was visiting . "I wish I could see the world on my own" thought Inca. "Then I could have my own adventures."

Inca stared out the window for the hundredth time that day and blew out a big sigh. Nothing was happening here. It was the same every day on the holidays. Then, out of the corner of her eye she saw a bright purple blob out in the garden.

Desperate for something . . anything . . interesting, Inca raced out the door. Out in the garden was a gigantic balloon. Quite how or why it got here was anyone's guess. Inca sat down to blow the balloon up and see how big it was. After about 10 minutes of huffing and puffing, the balloon was hardly taking shape - this balloon was huge! Inca set herself the challenge of seeing how big she could blow it up.

After what seemed like ages, Inca stopped. She simply couldn't get any more air into the balloon but it still looked like it could get bigger. Inca put a big heavy garden rock on the bottom of the balloon and stood back to look at her handiwork. The balloon was really massive, "If I could find some string" , Inca thought, "I could float away with it and find some places I bet even Maisie hasn't seen yet". She searched the garden and found the rope her mum used to tie up the tomatoes. Inca tied the rope to the balloon, held onto the other end, pushed away the rock and the wind caught the balloon.

Inca lifted up, up , up into the air. Carried on the wind, she could see her house down below. Inca looked out to see mountains in the distance and parks before that. She was floating towards them and she couldn't wait to see what was there. The wind bounced her around on it's warm currents, as she rose higher, she came across a squirrel in a tree.

"What are you doing up here?", asked the squirrel as it darted in amongst the branches. "Im looking for exciting new places to visit", said Inca. "This tree is pretty exciting!", exclaimed the squirrel "It gives me shelter and provides me with food". Inca looked at the tree, despite its size and beauty, it wasn't that exciting. Inca held tightly to the balloon as it lifted up in the air again "Thanks", she yelled down to the squirrel ,"but I’m looking for something a little more exciting!

The wind picked up and quickly flew her away from the city and her house. Despite how high up she was, Inca wasn't worried. The balloon looked like it could stay up for forever and Inca had eaten a big breakfast. She flew out over the parks she saw when she first lifted off, the wind started to slow down and Inca gently drifted towards the park grounds, even though she was up in the air, Inca spotted a mouse scurrying about amongst the leaves.

"Where did you come from?" said the mouse, "I normally hear little girls stomping through the bush from a mile away!" "I flew here on my balloon", said Inca. "Im looking for exciting places to visit". "The park's very exciting", said the mouse, "There’s food scraps left from the visitors to eat and I have a nice hollowed out log to live in". Eating food scraps didn't sound that exciting to Inca, she clung to the rope as the wind picked up and waved to the mouse as she took off again.

Nibbling on the grasses nearby, a kangaroo spied Inca out of the corner of her eye. "I heard you talking to Mouse", she said. “I can show you the really interesting places. Lots of space to jump around in and lots of flowers, bark and grass you can eat!”. Inca watched her nibbling on the grass. “The grass looks nice”, said Inca, “But I speak from experience when I tell you that little girls like cake more”

The winds picked up again and Inca snuggled into the rope as she left the coast and went out over the water. Everything looked clean and crystal clear from up here. Gulls, swung about in the wind just like she was doing, and soon she was out over open ocean. Down below, something huge and grey was in the water. Inca moved her grip to get lower down for a look.

Smashing through the waves and squirting water high into the air was a giant whale! “What brings you here on such a strange device?”, bubbled the whale. “Im looking for interesting places to visit” said Inca swinging from her balloon. “This is the best place”, frothed the whale “ It’s got cool waters, and more krill than a little girl could eat!”. Inca didn’t think lots of krill sounded like a good time and that water looked cold. She waved to the whale and swung her balloon back high into the sky . . .

Inca could see land in the distance and before long the winds carried her high over a savannah. She was in Africa! Even from her lofty perch, Inca could see and hear hundreds of animals prowling, playing, and making a general racket. Keeping her distance from the lions and tigers, she swung in on a pack of elephants for a closer look.

The giant elephant slowly plodded along the savannah. “What are you doing here little girl?”, it trumpeted. “I’m looking for interesting places to visit”, replied Inca. “We walk up to 50 Kilometers a day looking for water, you get to see lots of interesting things in Africa”. Walking that far didn’t seem like Inca’s thing so she waved goodbye and set of looking for someone else to visit.

At the tree line, a herd of giraffe were stretching their long necks to eat the leaves at the top of the trees. They jumped back in surprise when Inca dropped in on top of them. “Relax”, laughed Inca, “I'm not stealing your leaves, I’m looking for interesting places to visit”. “This is an interesting place for giraffes”, they exclaimed. “You look more like a monkey, perhaps you should visit them if you don’t like leaves”. Inca took their advice and headed into the jungle canopy.

Dropping lower to see through the thick jungle canopy, Inca came face to face with a monkey swinging through the trees. “That looks like a fine way to travel”, said the monkey, “Can I have it?” “I need it to visit interesting places and eventually, to get home”, said Inca. “Stay here”, the monkey said “lots of fruit in the warm months and we eat bugs when the fruit runs out”. Bugs were never on Inca’s menu so she said her good bye’s and shifted the balloon to carry her high into the the sky again.

The winds managed to carry her back over Australia but it was starting to get late and Inca had missed lunch. She looked down and saw a waterhole. Crickets were chirping and bugs swarming in the late afternoon sun. The wind had slowed down considerably, and below her, Inca was slowly overtaking the biggest snail her eyes had ever seen. She swung down for a closer look.

The snail was busy chomping on some decaying leaves. “Can’t stop to talk” said the snail “When you move as fast as I do, the food disappears before you get a chance to eat it’. “Im looking for interesting places” exclaimed Inca. “My place is interesting”, the snail said between mouthfuls, ”I carry my home with me so I never have far to travel!”. Inca took off into the sky, she wasn’t going to try and get inside a slimy snail shell for a look around thank you . . .

Basking on a rock in the last rays of the warm sun, a lizard watched as Inca flew past. "My spot is much more exciting. Plenty of rocks to help keep you warm and all the insects you can eat at the waterholes in the evenings". The lizard licked her lips in anticipation at the mention of the insects and shuffled over to a warmer part of the rock. "Er . . .insects aren't really my thing" said Inca, stretching out her legs to catch more air and rise up into the skies again. She really didn't want to eat insects. . . . .

Inca was starting to think about home and when she flew over the city, she knew she was getting closer. She could hear the city noises bubbling up from below and as she passed the tops of the city buildings, Inca saw two pigeons huddled in the last of the light.

“Enjoying the view little girl?”, they cooed, “soon, when all the people leave we’ll fly down and munch on all the lovely crumbs in the food courts and then snuggle up here for the night”. Eating crumbs and spending a windy evening on the top of a skyscraper wasn’t Inca’s thing. While the view was fantastic, it wasn’t for her. Suddenly she really wanted to get home, so she tilted the balloon again for one last push.

Inca got to her house just as Mum and Dad were getting dinner on the table. “We were starting to wonder where you were Inca”, said Dad. Inca jumped up to the table and proceed to tell her parents about her day and all the amazing places she’d been. She took them outside to show them the balloon but there was only the rope left behind. “Perhaps it’s gone to find another little girl to show the world”, said Dad.

That night Inca sat down and drew all the animals she’d met, cut them out and stuck them to the rope she’d used with her balloon, over her window. As amazing as all the places she’d visited were, she knew that each was best for those that lived there and even though she used to think nothing of it, there was no place like home for Inca. It was the best school holiday ever!
